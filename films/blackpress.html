The Black Press (1999)

About the Film

Spanning 170 years of American History, The Black Press details the role of African American-operated newspapers as voices for their communities, spreading important news, events, and achievements in a time when mainstream media did not represent them.

Many of these journals were underfunded and relied heavily on circulation and readership. However, not being sponsored through advertisements from major companies allowed the papers to report on the topics they wanted. These papers were admittedly biased because they recognized the prejudice within white-operated newspapers and wanted to focus on the Black stories not being told by these sources. Despite financial burdens and public pushback, black newspapers had a large impact. They helped to unite black communities across the country and inspire major events such as The Great Migration and the civil rights movement.

Journalistic Themes

  • What stories to print, what stories deserve to be told
  • Publishers as activists
  • Black papers were intentionally biased because they knew white papers were and would be, wanted to highlight black excellence rather than creating a neutral paper
  • Pre-civil war, surge in reconstruction, instrumental part in the great migration, through civil rights, lost power as historically white papers with more readership began to hire black editors and report on black stories
  • Other papers that advocated for black people, but black journals started to tell stories from first-hand perspective
  • Major contrast between depictions of black people in black papers and white papers, in white papers there were demeaning cartoons and unequal representation, black papers had cartoons that portray black people as heroes and told little known stories of black historical figures
  • Black Journalists were celebrated members of their communities

Historical Context

  • Was first broadcast during Black history month in 1999
  • Revealed history of black journalism that was not well-known or talked about as much during that time
  • Paid tribute to pioneering black journalists that created a space for black stories in the media
    • Showcased legacy of black papers like the Chicago Defender and how they laid the groundwork for more recent black journals
  • Also reinforces idea that black journalism was not mainstream and many black experiences were not highly publicized
    • Ties into current struggles within journalism for minorities
  • Minorities today face similar challenges despite progress
    • Unequal pay
    • Underrepresentation
    • More support for mainstream (white) media and papers

Contrasting Film: All the President's Men

Journalist-as-hero in a different way, in this case an unceasing drive to uncover “the truth.” The Black Press’s uncovering of the truth does not spout from some wide-spread conspiracy of hidden information, but the harsh reality of a racist world that simply refused to tell certain stories. Woodward and Berenstein were fighting against the government, but these reporters and publishers were fighting the entire country.
