Nightcrawler (2014)

About the Film

Nightcrawler is a thriller(dark comedy, drama) directed by Dan Gilroy that captures the fictional rise of Louis Bloom, a criminal turned video journalist who goes to morally questionable lengths to climb up the ladder and deliver graphic videos to a struggling news company.

Historical Context

Nightcrawler captures the contemporary landscape of media practice and consumer culture as well as the struggles of unemployment and the consequences of capitalism on the working class. Based on this setting they detail Louis Bloom, the protagonist as an anti-hero representative of advancement in American society despite adverse conditions. On the other hand Bloom represents greed in the industry as he is willing to reach his journalistic goals through intrusive means and even breaks the law to get certain shots. The film also details exploitation under capitalism exemplified by the character Rick and his relationship with Louis. Rick, who is desperate for a job, is forced to work under uncomfortable conditions and become an accomplice to illegal acts in order to make ends meet. The film makes commentary on the contemporary media and its relationship to the consumer in that our susceptibility to the sensationalization of fear and media contributes to this type of media behavior. This is exemplified by the demands that Nina places on Louis to get footage that will make headlines. Specifically, she calls Louis to capture crime in suburban instead of urban communities. Lastly the film makes commentary on the competitive nature of video journalists and warns of the potential consequences of this through the dramatization of Bloom and Loder. Bloom and Loder are constantly competing against each other to be the first at every scene throughout the film. This competition turns violent when Bloom sabotages Loder’s van in order to take him out of the industry. A historical figure that gave inspiration for this film was the photographer Weegee of the 1930’s 40’s. Weegee, Arthur Fellig, specialized in taking graphic photography of New York City crime scenes. Arthur, similar to Louis, was a freelance photographer who was known for being able to arrive at crime scenes shortly after they occurred.

  • Exploitation of Rick who is desperate for a job and is forced to work under uncomfortable conditions
  • How capitalism encourages competition within the media
    • Competition between Louis Bloom and Loder
  • Media Practice and Consumer Culture - News stations and their demand to show footage that will insight fear in their audience
    • The demands that Nina places on Louis to get footage that will make headlines
    • Footage often focussed on crime in suburban neighborhoods due to consumer demand
    • Parts of the story are chosen to be left out based on how much it ‘adds to the story.’
  • They’re narrative of crime leaking into the suburbs and Nina’s insistence on not mentioning it was a drug related crime to insight more fear into her audience.
