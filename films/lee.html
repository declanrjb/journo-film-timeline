Lee (2023)

About the Film

In an interview set in 1977, Lee Miller, model and photojournalist, recalls her experiences as a photographer for Vogue magazine during World War II. Lee reminisces on the time she spent in France prior to the war and her subsequent move to England after meeting her partner, Roland Penrose. As the war progresses across Europe and England is eventually pulled in Lee progressively becomes more involved through her photography. Initially used to promote protective gear and other war efforts through Vogue, Lee ultimately pushed her way to the war front in France and finally Germany. Over the course of the film Lee’s photography grew and changed after experiencing the war and the violence, sexism, and mental toll that it brought.

Themes of the Film

  • Sexism- Lee faced many obstacles when trying to work on the front lines to capture the war because she was a women
  • Photojournalism- this film showed how photojournalism is vital in informing the people of what is actually happening in the world
  • Cost/effects of war- the audienced learned about the mental health effects of War, as well as how deadly and violent war is
  • Family - Featuring close friends, partners, and her son, Lee is surrounded by family in every period of the movie and is greatly impacted by their presence and absence
  • WWII - Set primarily during WWII, Lee’s life and photography as well as all other characters are heavily impacted and shaped by the revenging war they are surrounded by.

How Journalism Works

Throughout Lee journalism is shown in the field where the audience sees Lee and her friend Davy taking photos. Traditional news rooms aren’t pictured and only a few short scenes allow the audience to view the England Vogue’s workspace. This creates a picture of journalism being fast paced, ever changing, and hard physical work.

Contrasting Film: Nightcrawler

  • Although both Lee and Nightcrawler focus on photojournalism, the ways in which these forms of journalism are portrayed and used within the films are starkly different.
  • Lee highlights how capturing the atrocities of the war were so important to inform people and raise awareness to certain issues
  • In Nightcrawler, Lou obsessively tries to capture the most gory and disgusting videos in order to make money
  • Lou in Nightcrawler manipulates scenes that he wants to capture in order to get the most intriguing shots.
  • Unlike Lou, who almost seems in awe of the horrors he films, Lee is deeply moved by what she captures and only cares about showing people the truth
  • Lee shows photojournalism in a positive light, important for showing the world harsh realities with hopes to foster change for the better
  • Nightcraawler highlights the corruption within the news/journalism world and critiques the exploitation of suffering to gain profit.
